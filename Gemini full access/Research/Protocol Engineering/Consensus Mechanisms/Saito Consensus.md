# Saito Consensus

## Overview
Saito is a Layer 1 blockchain protocol designed to solve the challenges of infrastructure funding and transaction scalability. Unlike traditional Proof-of-Work (PoW) or Proof-of-Stake (PoS) models, Saito incentivizes the routing and processing of transactions.

## Core Concepts
- **Proof-of-Transaction**: Nodes are rewarded for facilitating transactions rather than just performing hashing or staking capital. This creates an economic incentive structure that aligns network security with transaction volume.
- **Economic Security**: Security is derived from the work performed in routing data. This ensures that the network is protected by active participation rather than passive ownership.
- **Scalability**: By decoupling transaction processing from validation, Saito maintains high throughput without sacrificing decentralization.

## Relationship to Settlement
Saito acts as the finality and settlement mechanism for the [[UPI-Saito Settlement Layer]]. It provides a trustless environment for verifying and finalizing off-chain transactions, enabling a seamless bridge between legacy financial systems and decentralized infrastructure.
