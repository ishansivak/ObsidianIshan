# Fungi as Biological Networks

## Overview
Fungi, specifically mycelial networks, function as complex biological communication and resource transport systems. They act as the "internet of the soil," connecting diverse plant species and regulating environmental feedback loops.

## Key Properties
- **Mycelial Connectivity**: Extended hyphal networks allow for long-distance transport of water, nutrients, and chemical signals.
- **Environmental Sensing**: Fungal networks respond to local environmental stimuli, altering their architecture to optimize resource acquisition.
- **Information Exchange**: Chemical signaling through the mycelium enables communication between plants, potentially sharing information about pest attacks or nutrient deficiencies.
