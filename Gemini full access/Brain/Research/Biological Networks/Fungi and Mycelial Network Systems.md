# Fungi and Mycelial Network Systems

Fungi, specifically mycelial networks, function as complex biological communication and resource transport systems. They act as the "internet of the soil," connecting plant species and regulating environmental feedback via hyphal architectures. These networks exhibit self-organizing properties, enabling long-distance transport of water, nutrients, and chemical signals.

### Distributed Intelligence
Networks respond to local stimuli—moisture or nutrient gradients—reconfiguring their architecture to optimize resource acquisition. Chemical signaling within the mycelium facilitates plant-to-plant communication, modeling similarly to packet-switched network protocols.

### Bio-Inspired Mesh Networking
Applying mycelial growth patterns to mesh routing protocols allows for adaptive networks that prioritize survival and throughput at the local, neighbor-to-neighbor level.
