# Comparison to Mesh Networking

When comparing mycelial networks to human-made mesh networks (e.g., [[Meshtastic]], [[Reticulum]]), several parallels emerge:

- **Self-Healing**: Both systems rely on decentralized routing to bypass failures.
- **Dynamic Optimization**: Just as fungi optimize for nutrient uptake, mesh nodes optimize for signal strength and throughput.
- **Scalability**: Mycelial networks can span kilometers; mesh networks are theoretically limited only by the number of nodes and protocol efficiency.

Reference to Neil Gershenfeld's "Designing Reality":
Gershenfeld discusses the convergence of digital and physical fabrication. The idea of "programmable matter" and decentralized manufacturing echoes the way mycelium "manufactures" its own structure dynamically based on the environment.
