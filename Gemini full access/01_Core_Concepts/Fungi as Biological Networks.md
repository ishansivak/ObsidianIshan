# Fungi as Biological Networks

Fungi, specifically the mycelial network, serve as a compelling biological analogue for decentralized network architectures. These networks are characterized by:

- **Decentralization**: No central hub; intelligence and resource distribution are emergent properties.
- **Resilience**: The network can sustain damage to parts of its structure without failing, rerouting resources dynamically.
- **Resource Exchange**: Mycelia facilitate the movement of nutrients between plants and different areas of the forest floor, much like data packets in a mesh network.
